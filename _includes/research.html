Research

Neglected Diseases (NTDs) caused by parasitic worms (helminths) impose a debilitating health and economic burden throughout much of the world. These global "diseases of poverty" infect over 1.5 billion humans and exert their damage through a wide range of species-specific clinical manifestations. Parasitic diseases also constitute a major challenge to animal and plant health. The central ambition of our laboratory is to combine advanced molecular, genetics, and computational approaches to make discoveries that improve our understanding of parasite biology and our ability to diagnose and treat the infections they cause. This includes the identification and validation of new targets for drug discovery, the elucidation of new mechanisms of drug (anthelmintic) resistance, and the development of new tools for parasite manipulation. Our initial work will rely on the direct study of vector-borne parasites (Brugia malayi) and the leveraging of powerful model organism systems (Caenorhabditis elegans). We strive to place our work in the context of a growing appreciation and understanding of parasite genetic diversity. Detailed summaries of funded research projects will be outlined on this page.
